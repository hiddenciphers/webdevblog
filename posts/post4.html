Tafe Week 1

2024 Web Dev Blog - Tafe Week 1

Published on: February 11, 2024


This was my first week at Tafe in the virtual classroom environment. It has been a slow start but generally enjoyable nonetheless. We spent each class being introduced to the teachers & learning a bit about each other, while getting familiar with navigating the TDC (Tafe Digital Campus). All the teachers seem nice & very knowledgable in their fields which makes me excited to really get into it & learn as much as I can.


Monday is a shared class with the Programming students where we cover crossover ICT units such as:

  • Apply advanced critical thinking skills to work processes
  • Identify & evaluate emerging technologies & practices
  • Identify & resolve client ICT problems
  • Contribute to cyber security risk management
  • Work collaboratively in the ICT industry
  • Comply with IP, ethics & privacy policies in ICT environments
These are all essential topics to provide us with an understanding of how to operate professionally within an ICT environment. I'm excited to work through these modules & am confident I will gain a thorough understanding of each topic.



Tuesday is a Web Dev focused class where we will cover units such as:

  • Create a markup language document
  • Create & style simple markup language documents
  • Create responsive web layouts
  • Confirm accessibility of websites
  • Create basic databases
  • Apply a structured query language in relational databases
  • Produce server side script for dynamic web pages
These are the topics I'm really excited about! I can't wait to start working through these modules so I can begin to apply the knowledge to my personal projects! This is exactly why I enrolled in this course.


The teacher for these modules, Steve Graham, is a really talented & knowledgeable dev & I'm excited & honoured to be able to learn from him. Steve gave the class a sneak peak at one of his personal sites & it was glorious!



Wednesday classes consist of 3 units:

  • Transfer content to websites
  • Evaluate & select a web hosting service
  • Produce basic client-side script
These units provide us with knowledge of the essential industry tools & services used to manage the structure, hosting, deployment & maintenance of web projects. Most importantly the secure storage & transfer of sensitive data.


To my surprise, the teacher for these units is a skateboarding & 90s punk obsessed lady named Shanna Roper. Shanna is absolutely lovely & as we share 2 deep passions, skateboarding & music, I am so excited for her classes & to be able to learn from her years of experience in Web Dev.


Skateboarding & music have been the 2 main focuses for most of my life & as I shift my focus to acquiring new skills in this field I am so extremely greatful to have the opportunity to connect with & learn from Shanna! She even has a shared Spotify playlist for the class that we can all contribute to. Naturally, I obliged & added a handful of tracks I have been listening to recently & another handful of old favourites.



Thursday class is comprised of more exciting units such as:

  • Apply introductory programming techniques
  • Implement content management systems
  • Design website layouts
  • Implement search engine optimisations
The information contained within these units will ensure we are familiar with & comfortable implementing industry standard practices when designing processes & writing code.


The teacher for these units, Shaun Stummer, is extremely knowledgeable & has a wealth of experience working with a variety of programming languages. I really appreciate his teaching style & approach, which encourages us to jump in, play with the code, make mistakes & learn from the experience of practical exercises. We have started with a focus on Python fundamentals.



On Saturday, while doing some Python revision, I was feeling confident so I jumped straight into the first knowledge assessment quiz for Thursday's unit "Apply introductory programming techniques".


As I have about 6 months of experience working with Python from the Fintech bootcamp which I completed in 2023, I consider myself to be at an intermediate level with my understanding of Python, so I didn't hesitate to jump into the quiz to test my knowledge.


The quiz allows us to have 10 attempts at 13 questions & we must get all the questions correct to pass the unit. On my first attempt I got 2 questions wrong! These were on the topics of Python Data Structures & Python Tests which admittedly I have not spent much time learning about, though it prompted me to spend the next 10 minutes obsessively researching to refresh my memory & then I was able to retry the quiz & get those 2 questions correct.


I then spent Saturday afternoon further revising these topics by creating notes to store in my icloud drive which I then reviewed for the rest of the night as I was relaxing on the couch.


I woke up this morning & decided to include a new Revision directory in the structure of my blog project where I can create & store detailed independent pages dedicated to explaining concepts which I feel I need to deepen & solidify my understanding of by applying the Feynman Technique.


This new Revision directory will serve as a great resource for me going forward & I'm excited to expand my learning experience by contributing to it each week.
